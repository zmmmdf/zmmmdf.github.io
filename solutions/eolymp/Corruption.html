Corruption

Statement:

( The original statement can be found here )With the purpose of fight against a shadow economy a bank inculcated merging of the accounts of a company into one. For one operation 2 accounts are merged into one and bank automatically takes its own charge fee of % from the new account total for this operation. What is the most funds which could remain on the company' account? Each account has maximum charge of UAH before merging.

Input:

In the first line we have 2 numbers: amount of accounts and the fee percentage P.The second line of N numbers: the charge of each company' accounts.

Output:

With the purpose of fight against a shadow economy a bank inculcated merging of the N accounts of a company into one. For one operation 2 accounts are merged into one and bank automatically takes its own charge fee of Р% from the new account total for this operation. What is the most funds which could remain on the company' account? Each account has maximum charge of G UAH before merging.

Constraints:

Time limit 0.1 seconds
Memory limit 64 MiB
Show problem classificationgreedy-algorithm

Solution:

#include <bits/stdc++.h>
using namespace std;

int main()
{
    long long a;
    int p;
    multiset<long long> c;
    cin >> a >> p;
    p = 100 - p;
    int in;
    for (int i = 0; i < a; ++i)
    {
        cin >> in;
        c.insert(in * 100);
    }

    while (a-- > 1)
    {
        auto it_orig = c.begin();
        auto it = it_orig;
        long long s = static_cast<long long>(round(p * ((*it++) + (*it++)) / 100.0));
        c.erase(it_orig, it);
        c.insert(s);
    }

    long long r = *c.begin();
    cout << r / 100 << '.' << setw(2) << setfill('0') << r % 100;
    return 0;
}
